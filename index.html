What is OLEA?

OLEA is a digital waste oil collection and conversion platform designed to close the gap between everyday waste generation and sustainable fuel production.

Instead of treating waste cooking oil as a disposal problem, OLEA recognizes it as a strategic energy feedstock for sectors struggling to decarbonize — especially aviation and urban transport.

Why Waste Oil Matters

Environmental Risk

One liter of improperly disposed waste oil can contaminate up to 1,000 liters of freshwater, damaging ecosystems and sewage infrastructure.

Energy Opportunity

When collected and classified correctly, waste oil becomes a high-quality feedstock for biodiesel and Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF).

System Gap

The main challenge is not technology, but organization, traceability, and participation.

How OLEA Works

1. Waste oil is collected through OLEA-enabled systems.

2. The liquid is verified and classified as oil.

3. Each contribution is digitally logged for traceability.

4. Users receive sustainability points.

5. Users decide which sector their impact supports.

SAF & Biodiesel Explained

Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) is a drop-in replacement for conventional jet fuel and can reduce lifecycle CO₂ emissions by up to 80%.

Biodiesel is a mature renewable fuel technology used in road transport and public bus fleets, enabling immediate emission reductions.

Ecosystem Alignment

OLEA aligns with national and international sustainability initiatives, including efforts supported by the Turkish Sustainable Aviation Alliance (TSAA).
